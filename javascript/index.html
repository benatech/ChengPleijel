Cheng-Pleijel Canvas

This canvas implements the Cheng-Pleijel point of a quadrangle.

Suppose you want to build a tent with 4 stakes such that the amount of material used for the tent has the least cost, where would you put the central pole for the tent ? The answer is the put the pole at the Cheng-Pleijel point!. Let us show this with the help of this visualization tool.

Imagine the point O' (i.e. top of the pole) is situated at a certain height H above the flat surface containing the quadrangle ABCD (the tent stakes) and which projects down directly to a point O on the surface. By definition, the Cheng-Pleijel point (CP point for short) is the point where the sum of the areas of the triangles O'AB, O'BC, O'CD, O'DA are at a minimum value for a given value of H (default H=1). For an actual tent, gravity does deform the area somewhat, but does not affect the solution.

In the case of three points on the planar surface, it can be seen that the CP point is exactly the incenter (center of the inscribed circle) of the triangle form by the three points independent of the value of H. The first non-trivial case occurs with four points on the plane forming a quadrangle. If the quadrangle ABCD can be circumscribed about a circle, then the CP point is again the center of the inscribed circle for any H. If not, then the CP point actually changes its position for different values of H! Interesting cases occur when the height H approaches zero (or infinity), and the CP point converges to some limiting point.

In this canvas, you can experiment with different positions of the four points A,B,C,D, and height H, and observe how the position of O varies. To change a point's position, just press the mouse button on that point and drag it to a new location of your choice. The new location of the CP point O will automatically be computed on the canvas. The canvas grid size is 1x1 unit, and the height is just the length of H. The coordinates of the CP point O are given right below the height value. Coordinates of the vertices of ABCD are also supplied in the side tables, as well as the length of the sides. Be aware that if ABCD is not convex, then singularities can occur and if this happens, typing the R key will reset the canvas to its original state. You might want to zoom in on the canvas to obtain better mouse control for dragging points.
